## APPENDIX A: Clinical Interview Protocol (CCA-CIP)

### A.1 Purpose

Structured clinical interview for differential assessment of Cyber-Acoustic Convergence experiences.

### A.2 Administration

- Duration: 30-45 minutes
- Setting: Quiet, private room
- Materials: Recording (with consent), scoring form

### A.3 Interview Sections

#### Section 1: Demographics and Consent (5 min)
- Confirm identity and consent
- Document language(s) spoken
- Note sensory aids (hearing aids, etc.)

#### Section 2: Experience Description (10 min)
- "Describe what you hear in your own words"
- Location (internal/external/uncertain)
- Frequency and duration
- Content (if willing to share)
- Associated context (place, time, activity)

#### Section 3: Technology Context (10 min)
- "What devices do you use regularly?"
- "Where are you when experiences occur?"
- "Do experiences relate to device use?"
- "Do experiences change when devices are off?"
- "Have you changed devices recently?"

#### Section 4: Environmental Context (10 min)
- Background noise during experiences
- Room acoustics (echo, hum, ventilation)
- Time of day patterns
- Geographic location patterns
- Changes with environment (moving, travel)

#### Section 5: Clinical Context (10 min)
- Psychiatric history
- Current medications
- Sleep quality
- Stress level
- Substance use
- Medical conditions

### A.4 Scoring

| Domain | Score (0-3) | Interpretation |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Technology association | 0=none, 3=strong | Higher → investigate CCA |
| Environmental pattern | 0=none, 3=strong | Higher → investigate acoustics |
| Clinical factors | 0=none, 3=severe | Higher → clinical assessment priority |

## APPENDIX B: SPIA Susceptibility Screener (SPIA-SS)

### B.1 Purpose

Brief self-report screener to assess susceptibility to Signal-induced Pareidolia of Auditory Speech.

### B.2 Items (30 total, 3 subscales)

#### Subscale 1: Cognitive Style (10 items)
1. I often find patterns in random things
2. I hear music in environmental sounds
3. I recognize faces in clouds or objects
4. My mind tends to find meaning in noise
5. I often think I hear my name when it wasn't said
6. I interpret ambiguous sounds as speech
7. I have a strong imagination
8. I tend to overthink things
9. I notice subtle sounds others miss
10. I often wonder if I really heard something

#### Subscale 2: State Factors (10 items)
1. I am often tired
2. I don't sleep enough
3. I am under significant stress
4. I spend many hours per day on devices
5. I use headphones/earphones frequently
6. My environment is noisy
7. I work from home alone
8. I use video conferencing daily
9. I often have background audio playing
10. I multitask with multiple audio streams

#### Subscale 3: History (10 items)
1. I have experienced unusual perceptions before
2. Family members have had unusual experiences
3. I have been concerned about surveillance
4. I have had periods of high suspicion
5. I have changed phones/devices due to concerns
6. I have been diagnosed with anxiety
7. I have been diagnosed with depression
8. I have had trauma experiences
9. I work in a high-security environment
10. I have been a victim of harassment

### B.3 Scoring

- Each item: 0 (not at all) to 3 (very much)
- Total score range: 0-90
- Subscale scores: 0-30 each

| Total Score | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0-30 | Low susceptibility |
| 31-60 | Moderate susceptibility |
| 61-90 | High susceptibility |

## APPENDIX C: Rapid CCA Triage Tool (RCTT)

### C.1 Purpose

5-item rapid triage to determine if full CCA assessment is indicated.

### C.2 Items

1. Do experiences occur primarily near electronic devices? (Y/N)
2. Do experiences change when devices are powered off? (Y/N)
3. Are experiences associated with calls or connectivity? (Y/N)
4. Are experiences more common in certain locations? (Y/N)
5. Has technology context changed near onset? (Y/N)

### C.3 Decision Rule

- 0 "Yes" answers: CCA assessment unlikely to be informative
- 1-2 "Yes" answers: Consider CCA assessment
- 3+ "Yes" answers: CCA assessment recommended
